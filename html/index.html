About us

Barcelona Balboa Festival is an international festival organized by the BCNswing association, together with Gloria Garcia.

Here you will enjoy classes with personalized attention from international teachers with an innovative methodology that centres on the students.

You will have the best live swing music at parties and nonstop dancing (with no competitions). With good leader-follower balance to ensure maximum dancing opportunities for everyone.

There will also be time to enjoy the city and its attractions.

Casino Aliança del Poblenou

The Casino l'Aliança del Poblenou is a stunning historical theater nestled in the heart of Barcelona's Poblenou district. It's a popular destination for diverse events, ranging from captivating concerts and theatrical productions to energetic swing parties. The theater boasts a spacious dance floor, making it an ideal haven for Balboa enthusiasts.

The wooden floor provides an excellent surface for dancers, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable experience. Additionally, the high-quality sound system adds to the vibrant atmosphere, creating the perfect backdrop for an unforgettable evening of Balboa dancing.

Enjoy with respect

BCNSwing believes Swing is a live art form, and we wish to approach these artistic expressions with respect and appreciation. 
We believe that everyone has the right to enjoy these activities with complete peace of mind, and that everyone has a responsibility to build a safe environment and contribute to the well-being of the whole community.

 No type of violent, intimidating, sexist, racist, or exclusionary behavior will be tolerated under any circumstances. If you see or experience any improper behavior during the festival please contact the organization team immediately or the purple spot called Punt lila, which you’ll find by the check in table, specially created for this.

The organization commits itself to treat any report with respect, sensitivity and confidentiality, whether from the dancers, from our local scene or from other places. Also, the organization reserves the right to expel anyone who acts inappropriately.

Dancing is a very physical activity and as such, everyone is responsible for their own physical safety and that of others around them. So, practice safe dance floor movements, apologize if you accidentally bump into another person, do not offer unsolicited advice or instructions, nor perform aerials on the social dance floor.

Please also take special hygiene care and review your personal odor because it is not pleasant to dance with someone who has bad body odor, bad breath or wears clothes with excessive sweat. We ask that you bring spare clothes and take extra care of your hygiene in order to avoid unpleasant moments for the other dancers.

By following this code of conduct, we will generate a much more pleasant environment for everyone where we can enjoy dancing and personal exchange.

Transport

To get around Barcelona by public transportation, there are different types of tickets available:

T-Casual card: It costs €11.35 and allows for 10 individual trips, including transfers between the metro, bus, and train in Zone 1 within a 45-minute time limit. (Zone 1: Barcelona and surrounding cities.)

T-Usual card: Until the end of the year, it costs €20 and allows for unlimited individual trips with the same conditions as the T-Casual. It also includes the journey to or from the airport.

If you are planning to travel to Barcelona by car, the easiest option is to ask your hotel to reserve a parking space for you. There are also covered and outdoor car parks where you can leave your car, but they are generally a little more expensive than those at hotels. The cheapest option is to park in a free space near the Gracia district, but these are difficult to find. We also do not recommend using your car during the weekend if you are visiting the city.

From the airport, you can get to Barcelona by several means of transportation:

Metro: Line 9 south. It stops at both Terminal T1 and Terminal T2 and goes to Zona Universitària. During the journey, it has several connections with other metro, bus, and train lines. It will take you about an hour to reach the center. If you purchase the T-Usual card, this journey is included, but if you purchase the T-Casual card, you will need to purchase an additional ticket that costs €5.15 per journey.

Train: R2 Nord. The station is located at Terminal T2. If you arrive at Terminal T1, you will need to take a free shuttle bus that will take you to Terminal T2. There is a train every 30 minutes and the journey, to Passeig de Gràcia, takes about 30 minutes. The train ticket costs €4.60 per journey.

Aerobús: It departs every 5 minutes from both Terminal T1 and Terminal T2 and the journey takes about 30 minutes. It has several stops along its route and ends at Plaça de Catalunya. The single ticket costs €5.90 and the round-trip ticket costs €10.20. Tickets can be purchased directly on the bus.

Taxi: There are taxi stands at both terminals. The journey to the center of Barcelona will only take you between 20 and 35 minutes and costs between €30 and €40.

To get to the different festival venues by metro, the closest stations are: Llacuna or Poblenou (Line 4, yellow).
